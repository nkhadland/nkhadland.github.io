About Me

I am a planetary scientist interested in microbial ecology and leveraging those insights to inform astrobiological investigations. My expertise spans environmental microbiology and geobiology across various settings, with a particular focus on volcanic systems as analogs for early Earth and Mars.

With over six years of field experience, including extended campaigns in Iceland, I investigate how life establishes in extreme environments. In the laboratory, I specialize in low-biomass microbiology techniques (DNA extraction, qPCR, ATP analysis), complemented by computational pipelines in bioinformatics, machine learning, and geospatial data analysis.

I am currently a DoD NDSEG Fellow and deeply engaged in the astrobiology community, organizing events at the University of Arizona and participating in NASA early-career workshops.

Research Focus

Investigating habitability and biosignatures through field campaigns and advanced analytics.

Volcanic Habitability

Studying rapid microbial community establishment in newly emplaced lava flows (e.g., Fagradalsfjall, Iceland) to understand early colonization.

Microbial Dispersal

Evaluating the spread of microorganisms in terrestrial sandsheets as a planetary analog for Mars, focusing on hyperactive environments.
